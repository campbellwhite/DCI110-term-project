Network Analysis

Network Visualizations of Different Relationship Types in a Fictional World

This project used network analysis methods to explore the different relationship types that exist in the fictional TV series "The Boys". Techniques for this project included network diagrams and visualizations, measures of actor prominence and network cnetralization, and using models to determine how individuals form networks in the aggregate.

Familial Relationships

A rather small network is visble above representing the familial relationshios in this fictional world of "The Boys" TV series. There are several components, comprised of mainly dyads and a few triads. This network is very decentralized.

Romantic Relationships

This is another relatively small and disjointed network, again with several components. This is a visualization of the romantic networks in this fictional world. There is a larger spoke-like structured component, with several other dyads and triads. This network is also decentralized.

Business Relationships

The network of business relationships is by far the largest one of the relatonship types considered. This network is very dense and transitive, indicating that many nodes have relationships with many other nodes. Some nodes are more central than others, such as Starlight, Maeve, Hughie, and Butcher.

Vertex Attributes Represented in Network Visualizations

Familial Relationships: Superhero or Human?

This is the same familial network as above, this type with the nodes representing if their are a superheor or human. This is useful when examining why the network is structured he way that it is; are relationships shaped by whether someone is super or not? These being familial ties makes the grouping of superheroes or of humans less extreme; typically, it is not possible to choose the people to which one is related.

Romantic Relationships: For or Against Superheroes

Here, the romantic relationships and the respective characters are colored according to if they personally support or oppose superheroes. Compared to the last network (family), there does appear to be more gorupings of relationships shaped by attitudes towards superheroes. This is referred to as homophily, when nodes generate ties with other nodes who share similar charcateristics.

Business Relationships: Loss of a Loved One due to a Superhero

This network of business ties reflects whether a node has lossed someone that they cared about at the hands of a superhero. The premise of this series is humans who have been wronged by superheroes conspiring to bring them down; thus, it is expected that this attribute of the characters would influence the structure of relationships. It does appear to do so; the people who have lost someone because of a superhero seem to cluster together to some extent, and the superheroes themselves, not having lost someone, seem to also band together.

Reflection

Included above is only the preliminary steps of network analysis to which I was exposed in my Exploring Social Networks course. Visualizations are the first tools which can be used to discern basic descriptive information about networks. In this course, we also studied actor prominence and measure of centralization of networks. These elemnets further help to deteriine the most influential members of a network, who also likely shape othe rnetowrk ties to some extent. These measures serve to try and elucidate how network structure impacts vertices at the individual level.

Another dimension of network analysis is trying to determine how nodes at the individual level aggregate to form a network structure. This is done using Exponential Random Graph Models (ERGMs), that try to create the actual network based on nodal attributes, dyadic level predictors, and local structural characteristics. ERGMs produce probabilities of tie formation conditional on the network elements listed previously. A good ERGM will produce simulated network models that very closely resemble the actual network.
